# Curated Sources for Markov Learning

This list focuses on reputable, citable, and accessible materials. Prefer open-access when possible. Use these for lesson citations and further reading.

## Textbooks / Monographs

- Norris, J.R. Markov Chains (Cambridge University Press, 1997).
- Grinstead, C.M., and Snell, J.L. Introduction to Probability. (Open textbook) — http://www.dartmouth.edu/~chance/teaching_aids/books_articles/probability_book/amsbook.mac.pdf
- Levin, D.A., Peres, Y., and Wilmer, E.L. Markov Chains and Mixing Times (2nd ed., 2017). — https://pages.uoregon.edu/dlevin/MARKOV/ (open PDF)
- Kemeny, J.G., and Snell, J.L. Finite Markov Chains (Springer, 1976). (Some chapters freely available)

## Courses / Lecture Notes

- MIT OpenCourseWare: 6.262 Discrete Stochastic Processes — https://ocw.mit.edu
- UC Berkeley: Stat 150 Stochastic Processes (notes/videos vary by year)
- Stanford: CS109 Probability (relevant foundations)

## Articles / Papers

- Page, Brin, Motwani, and Winograd (1999). The PageRank Citation Ranking: Bringing Order to the Web. (Original PDF widely mirrored)
- Rabiner, L.R. (1989). A Tutorial on Hidden Markov Models and Selected Applications in Speech Recognition. Proceedings of the IEEE.

## Tools / Libraries

- NetworkX — https://networkx.org
- NumPy/SciPy — https://numpy.org / https://scipy.org
- markovchain (R) — https://cran.r-project.org/package=markovchain

## Notation and Typesetting

- KaTeX — https://katex.org
- MDX remark/rehype plugins — remark-math, rehype-katex
